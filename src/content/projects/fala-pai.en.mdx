## The Story

My father needed surgery that compromised his vocal cords and his ability to speak. He could think and understand everything, but speaking was extremely painful and hoarse.

Communication became exhausting — for him and for everyone around him. I built FalaPai in a weekend so he'd have a way to express himself with his own voice again.

## How It Works

The app converts typed text into speech using ElevenLabs, which generates audio indistinguishable from a real human voice. My father's voice was cloned beforehand so the output sounds like him.

Key features:

- **Quick phrases**: pre-saved buttons for the most common things ("Yes", "No", "Water", "Come here")
- **Free text**: type anything and it speaks it out loud
- **Offline**: cached as a PWA, works without internet for saved phrases
- **Installable**: added to the home screen, looks and feels like a native app

## Technical Decisions

### Why ElevenLabs?

I benchmarked 4 TTS APIs. ElevenLabs was the only one that sounded human enough that my father's friends didn't notice it was synthetic. The latency (~800ms) was acceptable for the use case.

### Why PWA and not a native app?

Speed of deployment. A PWA lets me ship in a weekend and update without going through app stores. For a personal tool used by one person, that's the right tradeoff.

### Offline-first

The quick phrases are stored in localStorage and the PWA manifest caches the app shell. The ElevenLabs calls need internet, but the most-used phrases can be pre-synthesized and cached locally.

## Impact

My father used the app every day for over three months during recovery. It was the most directly useful thing I've ever built.

It's currently in production and still being maintained.

## Why This Program

Working at Energia Pecém was the first time I saw up close what happens when code goes to real production. Not "production" in the sense of a todo app or e-commerce. Production in the sense of: if this breaks in the wrong way, someone could get hurt.

That changes how you think about delivery, traceability, and what happens after the deploy. And I realized that most of what I didn't fully understand was in that layer between code and operations. CI/CD, observability, infrastructure as code, containers in production. I used these things daily, but superficially.

With AI accelerating everything, writing code will become less and less of a differentiator. What will matter is understanding architecture, knowing what's happening in a system while it runs, and being able to make infrastructure decisions without depending on someone else. That's basically what this program is about.

## Curriculum

Two years with significant practical lab work. The modules I'm most interested in:

**Delivery and Infrastructure**

- Continuous Integration and Delivery (CI/CD)
- Version control and configuration management
- Automated infrastructure
- Container orchestration and cluster management

**Architecture and Cloud**

- Microservices architecture and scalability
- Cloud computing (lab)
- Serverless computing
- DevOps-Integrated Software Development

**Security and Quality**

- Secure Software Development (DevSecOps)
- Automated and continuous testing
- Monitoring and log analysis

**Management and Product**

- Agile Methodologies in Project Management
- Product Management
- Advanced Topics in Software Engineering

## Status

Started in April 2026. Early modules are still introductory, but I've already completed 3 advanced AWS courses through the Educate Canvas platform. The heavier labs on cloud, containers, and microservices are concentrated in the second year, with expected completion in 2027.
